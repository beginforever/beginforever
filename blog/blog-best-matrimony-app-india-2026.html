What the Big Matrimony Apps Won't Tell You

An honest look at India's matrimony platforms — what they do well, what the marketing doesn't say, and what you should actually look for when choosing where to search for a life partner.

✍️ Begin Forever Team 📅 May 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

We want to be upfront about something: we're one of the platforms being discussed in this article. We have an obvious interest in how this comparison turns out. You should factor that in as you read.

That said, we've tried to be genuinely honest here — about what the big platforms do well, about where they fall short, and about where Begin Forever fits in. We'd rather earn your trust with honesty than lose it with a biased comparison that's easy to see through.

"The best platform for you is the one that was built with people like you in mind."

Shaadi.com — The Giant

Shaadi is the largest matrimony platform in India, and that scale is genuinely valuable. More profiles means more options. The user experience is polished. The brand is trusted by families across generations. If sheer volume is what you need, Shaadi delivers it.

What Shaadi doesn't tell you prominently: their verification is largely optional and self-declared. Profile photos are not verified against an ID. The "verified" badge on many profiles means a phone number was checked — not that a human being confirmed the person is who they say they are. At their scale, manual verification of every profile would be prohibitively expensive, so it doesn't happen.

For many users, that's an acceptable trade-off. For women who have been deceived before, or for families who want certainty, it may not be.

Jeevansathi — The Established Alternative

Jeevansathi has been around for over 25 years and has a strong presence in North India. Their in-person verification centres are a genuine differentiator — and they deserve credit for that. The platform is functional, and the matching tools are solid.

The experience for users outside the Hindu majority, however, is noticeably thinner. Their blog covers Hindu wedding rituals, Hindu astrological matching, and Hindu community-specific advice — which reflects where their user base and their design priorities actually lie. Other faiths are present but not centred.

We're new. We don't have millions of profiles. We're a founding-stage platform with 300 verified members and a launch date of May 20, 2026. That's the honest reality of where we are.

What we do have: 100% government ID verification for every single profile, manually reviewed by our team before approval. No profile goes live without a human being checking it. No exceptions.

We were built for all faiths equally — not as a filter option, but as a founding principle. And we were built with safety for women as a primary design consideration, not a feature added after the fact.

Which Platform Should You Use?

If you want the largest possible pool of profiles and verification is not a primary concern, Shaadi is the logical choice. It has the most people, the most established brand, and a functional matching experience.

If you're in North India and want in-person verification options, Jeevansathi is worth exploring.

If you want absolute certainty that every person you interact with has been ID-verified and manually approved — and if you want a platform that was built with your faith genuinely in mind, not as an afterthought — Begin Forever is built for you.
